From Nina Butler, Australia: 

Hi John,

I  have been busy uncovering the life of Phyllis and Frederick Titheradge. I am pretty sure I have now identified a photo of Phyllis and her children taken in Oct 1917. Evidently she was known a s Aunty “Eff” and my Dad had labelled the photo as “Mum’s ( ie Grace Millicent ) sister Aunty ‘Eff’.”

The photo has writing on the back “To Mother - f - ( I think this is short for “from”) Eff Best Love....Oct 1917”
 
The photo shows a woman seated surrounded by 6 children. At first I could not match this family up with any of my Nanna’s sister’s families and It did not match Phyllis’s Titheradge family as there appears to be two or three girls and a set of boy twins. But further investigation reveals one of the girls is most probably a boy as little boys were dressed similarly to girls back then but careful examination shows he is wearing a pair of shorts under the dress....in line with some research I did quickly on clothing of little boys in 1917. The baby could be either so if I made him a boy he fits with Colin, born 1917.
 
The ‘twins’ I suspect are the two boys who were born very close together. Anyway – this photo shows a woman at the lowest ebb. So tired and stressed. The children are glum. If you look closely at the face of Thelma in both photos ( including the one I sent with Keith and my Nanna)I can see the child in the woman.
They are obviously very poor but have done the best they can to make the setting ‘nice’ by laying down a blanket and hanging a fabric over the old paling fence as a backdrop. I will attach this photo for you.
 
I have another unlabelled photo that I am trying to identify. I believe after all this research and staring into the faces in the images that this photo shows our two main characters – Phyllis and Fred Titheradge.
 
Also in the picture are, I believe, Harriett Florence and her husband to be Robert Coxon. In the front is my grand-mother, Grace Millicent with a pet parrot. I think the photo may have been taken when the two girls got engaged ( they both sport rings on their marriage fingers ) or when they got married – both were married in 1905. That would make my grandmother about 13 in the picture and I think this is consistent with the image.
 
I have found someone on Geni who is researching the Stewart family. He has a photo of both Harriett Florence and Robert Coxon on his site but I can only view small images that don’t enlarge. If I can match Robert Coxon to the image in the photo I have then I am fairly sure we also have an image of Phyllis and Fred as well – on careful looking I can see a likeness to the Phyllis in the photo with her children as well as to the one of grown up Thelma.....I think Thelma looked a lot like her Mum.
 
I have messaged the Geni guy but goodness knows if or when I will hear back. But I think I am correct.
 
The story of Fred and Phyllis has a really dark side ( and you will sense this in the image I am sending) and I will write it to you when I have it sorted.
Note – Fred is not in the image because he is in gaol – convicted on a charge of attempted rape.
 
The children of Phyllis and Fred Titheradge :-
 
*Thelma Phyllis Martha b. 1906 d. 1974 21 Aug, 1974 late of Blacktown NSW.
She married Joseph R. Brown in 1927 in Burwood NSW. Joseph dies in 1933. I can find no issue. Thelma re-marries in 1944 to Patrick James Callahan in Chatswood. He was born in Orange in 1906. They are the same age.
Patrick James Callahan dies 1968 in Surry Hills aged 62.
I can’t find any issue for her.
*Frederick Stewart b. 1908 d. suddenly in 1927 in Wellington. I guess the ‘Stewart’ part of his name comes from Phyllis’s name of Stewart.
*Leonard Austin b. 1909 d. 30th Nov. 1969 at Newtown late of Leichhardt aged 60 years. He marries Maude R. Leary in 1934 in Orange. I can’t find any issue for him as I can’t look up births after 1915.
*Keith Graeme George?]  b.1912 d. 11978 Married Edna M. Gibbs in 1935 – she lived in the same street as the Titheradges did in Wellington . They had a baby girl called Barbara and in 1938 they had a son, John Frederick in 1938 [added by Tidridge].
*Wildred Henry b. C.1914 d. 1975 aged 61 late of Baulkham Hills NSW.
*Colin b. 1917 d. 9th Dec. 2005 88 years late of Wurtulla – formally of Rocklea and Orange NSW . He married Eliza Priscilla Howarth in Wellington.
 
I have found a ‘Titheradge’ living in Wurtulla Qld. so I might send them a letter...what do you think? Colin is the baby in the photo.
 
Many of the Titheradge kids were good at school and talented in music. Wildred seemed to have made a bit of a living with his talents.
Will add more later as I write up a chronological story.
Hope this information is OK.
Nina
