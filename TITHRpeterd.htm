     Peter Dion Titheradge BBC Radio Producer of ''I'm Sorry I'll Read That Again"

 

Recently [2015] John Tidridge wrote to me and said "I am reading John Cleese's book "So, Anyway __ .". quite revealing, funny ... on pages 159 and 274 I noted a mention [very complimentary] of Peter Titheradge __ a BBC producer. ..Ann, tell us morel ... " So here is my response.

Peter Titheradge is the four times great grandchild of Daniel Titheradge and Rebecca Baker who started the Titheradge line. He is the grandson of the great Victorian actor George Sutton Titheradge, son of the playwright, songwriter and actor Dion Tilheradge and nephew of the famous actress Madge Titheradge. His surname is sometimes misspelt and erroneously appears as Titheridge. Professionally he used the name Peter Dion Titheradge and then after about 1968 it was shortened to Peter Titheradge.

Peter lived in London UK Peter's early career saw him following his father's footstep as a writer. Military Records show that Peter was a 2nd Lieutenant in the Royal Artillery in 1942. Peter's career took him to broadcasting and he worked for the BBC. Peter gets a mention in several books written by actors I broadcasters of the times; always the reference is positive and complimentary.

Comments include "Peter is pleasantly eccentric'

"Everybody's friend"

"A dear friend and mentor"
