This is how John Tidridge and Lois connected:
Letter from Lois J. Titheridge, Victoria, 3135 Australia 7 October 1994.

To

Mr. & Mrs. Liz Tyler (John Tidridge's daughter and son-in-law)

Dear Liz & Tuppy,

A friend from our Heathmont Baptist Church here where I also attend has given me your names so I cold make a request of you. (Her name is Sandy Sherwin, & I understand Sandy has known (?) your parents.)

My query is this:- My name is Lois Titheridge , and over recent years, with my cousin's support, we have been working on our family tree and there are a few missing links.

Our family originated in and around Winchester in the south of England, and we understand one or maybe more of our earlier families migrated to Canada, perhaps in the early or mid 1800's. Some of them spelt their surname Tidridge, but they all originated from Titheridge.

My friend Sandy Sherwin said she knew of "Titheridge" folk who lived in Canada, & I gather she knew of them through your families!

I am wondering if this is correct, and if so, would you oblige me by forwarding their names and addresses and approximate age would help.. One of the very earliest family members to move to Canada was a James Tidridge (Titheridge) & there could be descendants known to you.

We could then make contact with them and send them some information. Which would help. Our own family descended from Daniel Titheridge and his sons migrated here to Australia in the 1850's, & James was Daniel's brother moved to Canada. I would so much appreciate your help.

We are supposed to be enjoying spring weather at present, but it is still very wintry with rain & snow on the distant mountains. We live at the foothills of Mount Dandenong & a lovely area to live. We are 17 miles east of Melbourne our city.

We are very blessed in the fellowship we enjoy at Heathmont Baptist where we have a number of home groups for Bible study. We have a team of four pastors who give good teaching to the church family.

Will look forward to hearing from you.

God bless,

Yours sincerely,
Lois J Titheridge

John Tidridge & Lois corresponded for several years ... but then... NOTHING MORE.
