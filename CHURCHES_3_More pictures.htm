CHURCHES OF ALBERTA
 
 
 
 

OCTOBER 31, 2010

After some dithering the pictures have finished up on simple page layouts. No thought has been given to arranging by denomination; locations are not given. Just have a look and enjoy the many, many types of church buildings. It goes without saying the Christian belief is that the church is the people...so feast your eyes on the buildings!

The church pictured on the left is John Tidridge's first church. He attended for about 10 years, and was in the choir requiring initiation into the honorable tribe of washer women. The initiation rite was to be thrown from a gravestone into a nearby holly bush. He was too big to be thrown so had the opportunity to leap from the grave!

As a choir boy he also had his first cigarette.. and the last for several years. Although he did smoke at one time later in life. He overcame this habit with prayer and encouragement from his wife. His is grateful to the church for grounding him in the Christian faith which he now continues to practice in Canada, in his local Baptist church, and hopefully, the community and city around him

Edmonton Journal March 26,1960

ST. JAMES' CHURCH: Archbishop to officiate at St. James. Most Rev. H.H. Clark, primate of All Canada and Anglican Archbishop of Edmonton will officiate at services at St. Jame's [sic] Church, 118 Avenue and 56 Street. 

His visit coincides with the first anniversary of the dedication of the church building. It also is the fourth Sunday of Lent, known as Mothering Sunday, a day when Anglicans, according to ancient custom, return to their mother church and spend the day with their families.

Archbishop Clark will confirm 20 candidates and will celebrate the Eucharist at 9.15 a.m. Immediately after the first communion of the newly confirmed a light breakfast will be served.

At the 11 a.m. Service Archbishop Clark will preach and dedicate gifts and memorials and commemorate those persons in whose memory they have been given.

 There will be no church school so that every member of the parish family may have the opportunity of attending the services. Baby-sitters will make it possible for families with small children to stay for both services.

 Edmonton Journal December 1964

 St. James Anglican Church, 118 Ave and 56th Street will close its doors Dec. 31 after a brief history of 10 years.

 Bishop W.G. Burch says the church was forced to discontinue services because there are not enough people to support it and because of its close proximity to St. Mary's Anglican.

 ERROR MADE

 An error in judgement[sic?] was made when the church was built so close to St. Marty's which is six blocks away, said the bishop.

 St. James has been without a regular minister since last spring when Rev. Gordon Ingram was assigned to a west-end Anglican Church.

 Congregations from both St. James and St. Mary's will be consolidated into St. Mary's Anglican Church and the parishioners have been advised to this effect,the bishop said.

 “

We can't afford to keep both churches open”

 

 The final service in St. James will be held Dec. 27 at 11 a.m.

 No decision has yet been made as to the disposal of the property, said the bishop.

Tidridge notes: The building now [2016] houses the Highlands Masonic Lodge. Search is being made for a picture of the church. It is interesting to note the figure of ten years is quoted but that does not tally with the history given: it would appear the church was open only about 5 years.
