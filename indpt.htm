Peter Titheridge wrote in September 2016:

Anthony Robin Titheridge: Married to Rose Edith Harper 2nd August 1947 picture above.

At time of marriage he was a structural steel worker.
 
Spent most of his life as a furniture salesman leading to his own furniture shop.
 
During the 2nd world war he joined the navy. Visited Australia on a carrier. Famously was on Russian convoyes and received a belated medal from russia.
 
 
Became a Freeman of the City of London 1998.
John Tidridge asked Peter is he was aware that William Henry used the surname Tetheridge while he was in the Army. Peter was aware and apparently his name was incorrectly recorded.by the army and it remained that way until he was discharged.
October 1, 2016: From Peter...The Freedom of the City of London goes back many centuries and is bestowed on someone who has been brought up and or worked for many years in London. You have to be proposed by a livery company such as goldsmiths or silversmiths. Past advantages are being able to drive your sheep over London bridge and you were immune from being press ganged to serve on ships. London used to be locked at night but being a free man you could come and go as you wished.
There are many articles on the internet about being a freeman and its history. Just put in Freedom of the City of London.
 
Our name is open to mis-stating. Tidridge is aware of at least one mispronunciation of his name. It was the practise to parade police officers before they were sent out on their duties.  They were lined up and and their names were call by the sergeant and off they  went. One particular sergeant called our hero Titridge. Finally he decided to do something about it and refused to answer to that name.  There some interchange between Tidridge and the sergeant and the mesage was dispatched and received.  He was always called correctly from that time on.
