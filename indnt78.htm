JANUARY 2013 Some earlier discussion with Nathan had revealed that some one in his family had been a crack shot in the British Army. This remained dormant until John Tidridge was looking at some army records found on Mike and Ann Titheradge's website: Click here.

John Harry Tidridge seems to fit the bill. He was born in Cork, Ireland in 1883. Came to Canada in 1919. He must have joined the Lancashire Fusiliers at a private and left in 1919 at a captain. In 1915 The then Sgt Major was promoted to 2nd Lieutenant as Commandant of the Small Arms School at Hythe. He remained at the School until 1919. He was returned to his own regiment in June 1919 as a captain.

The photograph is one of many received from Florence Haven, John Tidridge's cousin, New Jersey, USA. On the back the words Irish cousin are inscribed, when the photo was sent to England a note indicated, with a question mark, 'Francis Tidridge's brother'. Francis indicates a male, (trust me on this one) however (!) you will note a Frances (see family tree above) has a brother John Harry. The photo was taken by Paris Studio, 64A Patrick Street, Cork. The young soldier, at his (apparent) young age is already sporting marksman crossed rifles... and corporal's stripes.

From London Gazette

 

13 November 1915 Sgt Major John Harry Tidridge to 2nd Lieutenant
20 June 1916 John Harry Tidridge graded for purpose of pay as assistant instructor at school of musketry and temp lieutenant while so employed
22 July 1918 Assistant Instructor Lt J H Tidridge Lancashire Fusiliers, to be seconded and to be temp Captain while so employed.
10 May 1919 School of Musketry (Hythe) relinquish temporary appointment of Captain Lt J H Tidridge Lancashire Fusiliers
Lt J H Tidridge to be temp Captain from 6 June 1919 to 22 July 1919
