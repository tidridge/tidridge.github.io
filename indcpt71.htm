January 23, 2009 : Greetings from the bottom of the world.

My name is Craig Titheridge (b 1971 in Christchurch, New Zealand) and I have been doing a bit of research into our family tree over the last 12 months. I can successfully trace my tree back to John b 1643 but still have a few gaps to fill in. There are some other Titheridges here in New Zealand that I cant quite link into our family tree, I have done their tree back to Thomas who was born c 1774 and married Jane Tee 25 Sept 1803.

The area I am mainly interested in is the descendants of John b 1669 who married Ann Brewer. There is a bit of info on the web but I'm after something more complete. Can you possibly help me??? I have our complete tree in PAF5 format, if you would like I could email it to you. I also have a Word document available which traces direct descendants only between John b 1643 and myself.

The Titheridge family is alive and doing well in New Zealand, there is myself, brother Simon, sister Kirsten, my parents Tony and Margaret, and numerous other relatives. Also the other Titheridges which appear to have around 20 living descendants. Any information at all would be helpful, particularly military history (already have quite a bit) in relation to military medals and how the family actually got here, ship names, dates etc.

I have also emailed mike@titheradge.fsworld.co.uk but it has been a couple of months now with no reply. I'm finding a little bit of info from meeting fellow Titheridges on Facebook but nothing useable.

Looking forward to hearing from you.

Your distant relative :) Craig Titheridge

Gidday guys,

I haven’t done any family tree stuff for a long time. I did however go down to South Canterbury in the weekend and stay at Geraldine. I went and visited all the very old churches and graveyards in the area trying to find any trace of the descendants of Thomas c 1774-1846 who ended up in the area. I couldn’t find anything but did get a few good church photos.

St Anne,s in Pleasant Valley was the first church in South Canterbury opening in 1863.
Church of Holy Innocents at Mount Peel opened in 1869.

These being the only churches in the area for quite a few years would probably mean that James Titheridge 1840-1923, Eliza Rossey c 1837-1909, William Titheridge 1866-1895, Louisa Titheridge 1877-1907, Nellie Matilda Johnson 1879-1967, Albert George Titheridge 1903-1968, Maytel Geraldine Titheridge 1911-1994, Leslie Edward Titheridge 1902-1972, Amy Titheridge c 1894-1965, Maisie Geraldine 1917-, Elizabeth McDonald 1876-1958, and William McDonald 1875- probably attended one of these churches.

I have made a few contacts and have got a few more things to follow up on but at this stage nothing to report.

The Church of Holy Innocents was actually very impressive (and I'm not a “churchy” guy), beautifully maintained by descendants of the original land owner. Some of the headstones were unreadable, the wooden ones gone all together but a copy of all the original records including burial plots was inside.

Cheers guys, if anything else surfaces I will let you know

Craig Titheridge
