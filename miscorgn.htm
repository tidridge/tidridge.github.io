FAMILY NAME OF TITHERIDGE

From a chart sent to John Tidridge by Neil Titheridge of New Zealand, Neil indicated that the chart originated in Ireland and therefore perhaps, some blarney might be incorporated. Part says that the first recorded spelling of the family name is shown to be that of Francis Tedderidge, December 16, 1677.

Our first recorded name goes back to the 1500's, however...This unusual name is of English locational origin, from a place called, "Titheridge" believed to have been situated in Cheshire. It is an example of a not so uncommon phenomenon in medieval England, a "lost" village, one that has disappeared from the maps.
Whole villages were "cleared" and their population dispersed to make room for sheep pastures and there were also natural disasters like the Black Death of 1348 and accidents of war that caused villages and hamlets to "disappear".
The meaning of the placename is "Tidhere's ridge from the Old English pre-seventh century personal name "Tidhere", meaning "festival spear" and "hrycg" back, or ridge.

Annie Sophia Titheridge was christened on the 16th August 1857, at Holy Trinity Church, Paddington.
Family names as hereditary surnames did not come into general use until after the Norman Conquest of 1066. The Normans introduced National Taxation to England which they called the POLL TAX (Poll=head), in consequence there was a need for surnames for identification purposes

The first recorded spelling of the family name is shown to be that of Francis Tedderidge, christened 16th December 1677, St. Margaret's, Westminster. During the reign of Charles II, The Merry Monarch, 1660-1685.

Scotland, Ireland and Wales obtained formal records later than England, and this is reflected in the recordings. All surnames of every County have been subject to changes owing to dialect, Civil War, and plain poor spelling.

In a letter from Roy Daysh to John Tidridge, dated June 17, 1985 as to the meaning of the name, "I think it is a form of RIDGE - "a dweller on the ridge", and TITHE, presumably a particular ridge on which tithes were paid to the church".

From a letter, date unknown, indicating that it was once Tiedrich, a German name.

 

 

 

 

FAMILY NAME OF TIDRIDGE

Name change..an important part of the Titheridge-Tidridge saga..

The Tidridge variation of the surname appeared in the nineteenth century.
Henry Titheridge (1815-1868) who was born in Bishops Waltham and his wife Ann (Newell) had eleven children. Although he was born as a Titheridge his surname became written as Tidridge, and this is the surname he passed on to all his children and future generations.
It is also thought that his father William Titheridge(1775-1851) may have died a Tidridge.
