You are quite right - the name TITRIDGE does not in fact exist. There is however a reference to it in the British 1881 census. where an Albert TITRIDGE, aged 13 years, an under shepherd, born at Bishops Waltham, Hampshire, is shown as living as a boarder with Richard MAIDMENT, a shepherd, and his family at Rowhay Farm, Owslebury, Hampshire. (Richard is not related to me , but is related to a fellow MAIDMENT researcher.)

I have checked the 1891 census for Hampshire, which I have on CD rom, and also the national 1901 census, which is now available on the Internet, and in both cases Albert's surname is shown to be TITHERIDGE. The 1881 entry is therefore clearly a mistake. There are no entries for TITRIDGE in the indices of the Hampshire 1891 census, or the British Vital Records. Albert is the only one in the British 1881 census and the world wide Mormon IGI / census site.

As a matter of interest, a check of the 1901 British census reveals a total of 229 TITHERIDGEs, 28 TIDRIDGEs and 11 TITHERAGEs.

For the information of your members, the 1901 British census on the Internet is now working well after a troublesome start. Whilst there is a charge for viewing and downloading the actual census entries, the following information can be viewed and downloaded free of charge. Name, age, where born, administrative county and civil parish where living at time of census, occupation. Try www.pro.gov.uk then click on 1901 census online, then name search. Another very useful pay as you go site, opened on the first of this month, is that for the British births, marriages and deaths indices 1837 - 2001. www.1837online.com
