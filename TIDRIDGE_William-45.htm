The Cork Tidridges leading to New Zealand

William Tidridge (1845-1929), son of Henry Titheridge (1815-1868) and Ann Newell (1815-1873), came to Cork as a member of the British Army and in 1882 he married Mary Baker Brown (1856-1939) the eldest of six children of John Brown (c.1831-1909) and his wife Jane Dunlop (c1833-1912). When he left the army William became sexton at the Church of Ireland (Anglican) church of St. Mary Shandon in Sunday's Well, a suburb of Cork City where he died on 29th September, 1929 and is buried in St. Finbarr's Cemetery, Glasheen, Cork.

William and Mary produced ten children :-

1. John Harry Tidridge (1883-1918) married Evelyn Davies (1889-1989) and emigrated to Canada and,it is thought, settled in Windsor, Ontario. They had two sons, William (1916-1978) and Lionel (1912- 1980).

Lynne Tidridge (1953-?) married Bobby Grinder and lived in Scotch Plains, New Jersey) and
Judith Tidridge (1955-?) studied ballet and married into an Italian family.

Perhaps Bill and Shirley lived in New Jersey but it is thought that they moved to Arizona (Cottonwood?) following Bill's retirement.
