 1971 Edward H Tidridge
New Mexico USA
Tidridge, Edward H., 86 passed away late Saturday evening following several weeks illness. Mr. Tidridge, retired, had been a resident of Santa Fe for the past 11 years, coming to Santa Fe from New York. A member of the St. Francis Cathedral Parish. He is survived by his wife, Helen, two daughters, Mrs. Franklin (Helena) Woff of Upper Montclair, New Jersey, and Mrs. Frances, wife of Doctor Brian Moynahan of Santa Fe. Eight grandchildren and four great grandchildren. Two brothers, George of Bournemouth, England, and Sidney of Totten Hants, England, one sister, Mrs. Lou Price of Bournemouth, England. Parish rosary will be recited at 7:30 P.M. this evening at Memorial Chapel. Funeral Mass will be celebrated at 10 A.M. Tuesday from St. Francis Cathedral with interment to follow in Rosario Cemetery. Arrangements through Memorial Chapel Mortuary

 

16 October 2008 Martha Lou Tidridge
Hartford Courant (US paper)
Martha Lou Tidridge, died Friday (October 3, 2008). She was the widow of Edward J. Tidridge. She was born Oct. 3, 1922 in Fairmont, WV, daughter of the late Harold and Mamie Scatterday. Martha was a former resident of Clinton and Norwalk, and was employed by SNET as a service representative at the Westport office until her retirement. She is survived by her daughters, Jane Campbell and her partner, George Donahue of Enfield, and Terry Shemansky and her husband Bob of Illinois. She also leaves six grandchildren and eight great grandchildren. Besides her husband Ted, she was predeceased by a daughter, Barbara de Pierne. A Memorial Service will be held on Saturday October 18, 11 a.m. at St. Mary’s Church of the Visitation, 54 Grove Street, Clinton.

 

15 November 2003 Frances Moynahan McKinney (nee Tidridge)
Alberqueque Journal (USA)
Frances Moynahan Mckinney, 84, of Santa Fe died Friday after a short illness. She was a graduate of Cathedral High School in New York City. She was a ballroom dance instructor with the Arthur Murray Dance studios and traveled extensively with Mr. Murray and his wife Katherine. She served with the Altar Society at St. Francis Cathedral and with the St. Vincent Hospital Auxiliary for more than 50 years. She was also involved with the Villa Therese Catholic Clinic and the Museum Board of New Mexico. She was preceded in death by her parents, Ellen and Edward Tidridge; sister, Helena; brother, Edward; and first husband, Dr. Brian Moynahan. She is survived by her husband, Ralph D. McKinney; son, Timothy Moynahan; and many other relatives and friends.
