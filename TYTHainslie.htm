In February 2018 or thereabouts Ann Titheradge mentioned she had seen a newspaper report that Ainslie had been killed in a motor cycle accident in the UK. This appeared to be contrary to two other reports noted below 

 

http://canadiangreatwarproject.com/searches/soldierDetail.asp?ID=19899 and

 

 

https://www.cwgc.org/find-war-dead/casualty/389006/tytheridge,-ainslie-burton/

 

both of which reports, at least the first one does, of Ainslie dying as the results of wounds. The date of death is the same as quoted in the newspaper report as are the details of his rank and in what area he served.  Tidridge thought this to be strange, so he contacted the CWGC and gave them this information.

Rightly so, their very polite staff member required written proof. All Tidridge could was to point her to the British Newspaper Archives site [Tidridge had actually gone to this site and for a 'free bee' had managed to confirm the newspaper and name and date of the accident but he could not bring it up to a readable size.] Incidentally nor could CWGC. Ann Titheradge was involved in the conversation and I am hoping she was able to provide more information.

The manager of the branch became involved because the initial staff member thought I was implying some inaccuracy with the records.... He indicated he had no problem with the two reoprts as they covered the death of Ainslie.I wrote back explaining my motives... simply wanting the get the facts..

And so it remains a mystery...I did say in my last letter that if I was the manager I might do a little more research...but who I am....so, the mystery remains.
