Highlands has a new library… John Tidridge 2015

And, so do, the communities of Montrose, Bellevue and Virginia Park… and if one wanted, one could add Mount Royal as well...

The new, imposing building sits at the northwest corner of 67 Street and 118 Avenue. It beckons to old and new customers, and people interested in viewing the structure to come and take a closer look. To the surrounding neighbourhood it might be saying, "Look at me, I'm new... can you keep up with me?" It is obvious libraries contain books! It's just like a restaurant serves meals… but it is the service as well as the product that makes one want to return.

On August 11, 2014 a long, interesting conversation was held with the new manager, Joanne de Groot [a former adjunct professor at the U of A], and the Community Librarian, Anne Bechard. It is safe to say, 'All is well with the branch'! Enthusiasm and a desire was shown by both ladies, to provide the absolute best service, programs and projects that would best serve their clientele. It was a little early to have everything in place, but research was under way to understand the demographics of the library’s influence and to meet those needs.

Both are aware of the diversified nature of the area. Bechard had already made contact with several people, 'in the know' to discover the needs. de Groot admitted there were certain things as manager she was required to do, but she had much leeway to be creative and to produce programs and events relevant to the Highlands area. She intended to take advantage of that fact.

Old and new library programs are going ahead, covering a multitude of topics: it would be safe to say, if there was a need, with a reasonable number of people interested, all kinds of new and stimulating activities could/would be arranged.

About half of the former staff has returned, together with, of course, half that are new! Drop in and meet the staff! Borrow a book or a tape or just drop in and tour!

Environmentally speaking the building has passed the Leadership in Energy and Environmental Design standard. It is one of the first libraries to do so. More information can be found on the Library website.

The branch is approximately 11,800 square feet over 2 floors, making it 2,200 square feet larger than the old branch. Approximately 30,000 items are available for borrowing: enlarging the 'old' collection by over 18,500 items. In addition, there are 18 public computer stations, a larger children’s area, a bigger program room, an expanded reading area for adults. And, a dedicated teen space, a quiet study and reading area, a second floor mezzanine, together with new technologies and devices to promote making and creation in the branch.

History of the library:

"The EPL Board minutes for December 15, 1960 indicate that "the branch library to be established in north-east Edmonton should be known as the Highlands Branch Library. This early designation is necessary so that the Catalogue Department can prepare the book stock that is being acquired for such a branch library." Note that the library was named for practical reasons (acquiring and cataloguing of the collection) before an exact location was determined. It is not until November 21, 1961 that the decision is made to locate the Highlands branch at "the existing house at location (b)" - 11818-67 St." Or is that 11806 67 Street. [in 52 years the library moved about 60 feet!]

In 1963 the branch moved into a cottage at 8606-118 Avenue and was known as the “little house library”. A newer building, located at 6710-118 Avenue, was constructed in 1964. Since that time, the areas population as grown and its needs related to library services has changed, hence the new building, which opened in 2014. During the construction of the new building, services were provided at a temporary building at 66 Street and 118 Avenue.

The conclusion: The library is in good hands…visit and enjoy!
