Claus LUECK Bench
 [72 Street and Ada Boulevard]
 
The Lueck Bench is situated on the south side of Ada Boulevard in the Concordia University area, around 71 Street. It is and will continue to be, a busy area with lots of students and residents taking advantage of the splendid walking area. The photograph shows [barely!] some of the homes south across the North Saskatchewan River. 

 

On 2021-02-05 1:27 p.m., Karen Lueck wrote: Hello John, My sister me across your photos of my late husband's bench that I had the plaque made for. That's wonderful it made it onto your page  and I see that you're wanting more information, so I'll happily oblige!

Claus lived with his parents and siblings (he was the youngest of 4)  in a house just below 75th Street, on the side of the hill adjacent to  Highlands golf course. It was one of only three there that were owned by the City of Edmonton and whom they rented from.

I'm not sure exactly what age he was when they moved in but he did  attend Virginia Park Elementary, Highlands Junior High and Eastglen  High schools.

He told me many stories of growing up there and how much he loved it. He had many friends in the neighborhood who lived on the actual street (75th) and they called themselves the 75th street gang (not a criminal gang; just as in 'the gang').

They played games such as hide & seek, kick-the-can, etc and climbed up and down the nearby staircases. He would describe it as having the river valley as his backyard, along with his siblings. He and his buddies even climbed over the fence to the golf course and snuck in a  few practice shots from time to time.

 I'm attaching a photo of a painting his second oldest sister did of > the actual house they lived in. She painted it before the family had to unfortunately, move out as the City wanted to tear down the homes to further develop the parkland.

Claus was quite upset about them having to leave since he loved living  there so much but at least got to finish his last year of high school while still there.
 

 We went walking, bike riding and driving down Ada Blvd many times and  he'd show me where the house used to be. He was sadly, diagnosed with aggressive lung cancer in mid 2015 and as the plaque indicates, passed away December 11th of that year at the age of 58. I thought it would be the perfect tribute to honor his memory.

If you'd like any further information, please don't hesitate to ask.

 Sincerely,
Karen Lueck
