Old Age

(Written on October 2007 as I watched my friend Bruce McLean get out of my car and head to Shopper's Drugs)

Old Age! What a bummer!
Just like the last days of summer.
Aching back, wonky knees,
Slightest chill and we start to sneeze.
Walking is a continuing pain.
And what for? There is no gain.

Accept it? To give in would be a sin!
We must face aging with a grin!
Young people would just laugh and say,
Ah, but they've had their day.
But us older ones know it's not true
And really, to give us our due,
Our spirits are great, still in their prime,
We just know it's not the time
To concede to age!

OCEAN

What is this power that draws me to your shores?
Time and again I must come back for more.
Today you are a sea of sparkling diamonds,
As the sun shines down upon your gentle vista.
Change comes, the overcast sky causes you to look foreboding,
Your colour turning a dark and murky slate gray.
Huge waves whipped by the wind come crashing to the shore,
I do not venture close to you today.
Again change comes, children playing in your gently lapping waves,
Carrying pails of you to build sandcastles on the shore.
What is this power that draws me to your shores?
The Creator' Hand that brings me back for more.
