2604631 (formerly 27877) Alfred Charles TITHERIDGE (1893-1937) Occupation cowman. Next of kin: Mother, Lydia Titheridge, Spring Lane, Swanmore, Hants. Attested 11 December, 1915 aged 22 years 11 months. To Army Reserve 12 December 1915. Mobilized 17 November 1916, served in 5th and 3rd Battalions. British Expeditionary Force, France 9 June 1917 to 5 August 1918. Received *Gun Shot Wounds 9 October 1917 and multiple Gun Shot Wounds's legs and back 19 July 1918.

Discharged as no longer fit for Army Service 15 March 1921.

* Google gives several meanings, but this is what is it generally understood the letters stand for.

35147 Harry Percival TITHERIDGE Joined the Guards Machine Gun Regiment as number 8462 on 29 June 1918, aged 19 years 1 month. Next of kin: Mother, Mrs L R Titheridge, 115 Obelisk Road, Woolston, Hants. Posted to the 5th (Reserve) Battalion 27 November 1918 and discharged services no longer required for the purpose of joining the Navy.

He was sent to the Royal Navy Barracks at Portsmouth but failed to pass examination for Engine Room Artificer and was sent home to 'Glen Lyn', 115 Obelisk Road, Woolston, Hants with a recommendation for re-examination at age 21. No overseas service.

22545749 TIDRIDGE, J.W.F. son of Mr. and Mrs. W.S. Tidridge of Totton, Hampshire UK. now husband of Maureen of Edmonton, Canada.

John enlisted on April 13, 1953 and undertook training at Caterham Barracks. After completing basic and Field Training at Pirbright, Hampshire and Pickering, Yorkshire he was moved to the 1st.Battalion in London on February 3, 1954. He, along with the battalion, moved to Germany (BOAR) October[?] 2, 1954. His initial term of service finished April 12, 1956.

On August 7, 1956 John was recalled to his regiment, serving with the 3 rd Battalion during the Suez Crisis. He was demobilized on January 19, 1957. This short stint of service took him to Malta, Cyprus and Egypt. John concluded his service as a Lance/Sergeant.
