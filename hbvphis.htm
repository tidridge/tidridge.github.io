54th HIGHLANDS BAPTIST CUB PACK

 
 
 
 
 

 

Change in plans... from time to time items of history come to light... as in the case of the 54 th Edmonton Cub Pack. As items are discovered they will be inserted on this site

 

Sadly, Leaders Brian Traynor (left) and David Twigg (right) have died very early in life. The leader in the middle, John Tidridge is still going relatively strongly, but retired from the cubs, at 79 [April 2014].

Some one has said what a stern looking bunch of leaders and cubs...perhaps that's the way photos of cub groups were taken in the 1960! The Pack was formed in the 1950's under the leadership of Ken Jenkyns who later had helpers Walter Rideout and Bob [?] McVee. The group was sponsored by the then Highlands Baptist Church and was an 'open' group meaning any lad who wanted to join could... The group closed in the early 1970's when the church embarked on another youth program, Brigade Boys.

Tidridge began his term in 1961 and lasted[?] until 1971. He had been nodding his head politely as his wife and a friend were talking...he realized later that he had volunteered to help with the cub pack. It became obvious after a couple of meetings the 2 leaders had reached the end of their tether and wanted out. Bob McVee did stay on for a while though. In addition to Traynor and Twigg, Glen Holland helped for several years and also Fred Coffey. The late Mike Dronyk came to camp with us... and made excellent early morning coffee... he was a Transit operator at the time and 5.30 morning start was old hat to him.

Suffering from foot in mouth disease Tidridge had said at a meeting he did not want any women leaders... women leaders were common place back then but Tidridge wanted men... and he was fortunate to get the ones he did. Eventually the women forgave him when he was able to make his case clearer! He wanted the dad's involved. Having said that no group runs without the help of the parents and supporters. The Weetmans, Nowickis, Hewkos, McPhersons, Prices [Earl typed the Newsletter] , Welshs, James', Mr. Foth who supplied excellent bedding plants for fund raising, ...and the list could on.... There were bottle drives [eliminated because people might get the wrong impression with the assorted pop and other types of bottles stored [for a very short period of time] in the pastor's garage, wire coat hangers drives...

Some of the lads in the photograph...there are a least 2 Welsh boys, John Palinchuk, John Byron [now an Engineer] Jim Hewko, a James boy, Barry Nolt[?], Jim Sherwin[?] a Rudko ...perhaps they can be named by others... or, themselves. For those interested the stripes on the left arm recognized a leadership role... 1 stripe a seconder, 2 stripes a sixer...these two lads were to work together to supervise a six of, yes 6 boys. The senior sixer had 3 stripes. It might be said by some, the cubs were run on a military concept... no argument there!

In spite of their grim looks it's a safe bet they did enjoy themselves! There were 2 trips to camp at Skeleton Lake, various prized for being the best six [a sub-grouping in the Pack]. Prized included a trip in the Police private aircraft,trips to the Police station, a local radio station, the water cleansing plant, electrical safety and a couple of Jamborees [ a gathering of all the local Packs for an evening of games and skill testing.. the list goes on! Guest speakers were called in and gave interesting talks...the police canine unit came, a First Nations chief spoke to us...

The 54th Highlands Baptist Cub Pack…. By John Tidridge

 

Russ Knight says he is seated on is brother Grady's left, who is seated directly above the boy in the coloured shirt.  Rudko family report: Third from right Mike Dronyk, Rick Hart is to his left, second from the left Jim Werenka. Brent Rudko, first boy, second row, on the left.

DYB DYB DYB followed by We’ll DOB DOB DOB echoed through many a community in the 1950’s and 60’s…and in some cases into the 80's and 90's. To those in the know the seemingly gibberish words signalled yet another opening of a cub pack meeting. Groups met in any hall where they were sponsored. What do they mean? The Leader urged the lads to 'Do Your Best', and their response was, 'We'll do our best'.

There were 'open' and 'closed groups: 'open' meaning the only requirement to join was that the lad was of age… 8 years old. 'Closed' implied you had to belong to a specified organization before you could join. 'open' groups far exceeded 'closed groups and to this end Lord Baden-Powell, the founder of the Scouting Movement would have been well pleased.

The 54th was first headed up by Ken Jenkyns. Leaders and assistants along the way were: Walter Rideout, Tom McVeigh, Glen Hollands, Brian Traynor, Davie Twigg, John Tidridge and others!

Ladies played an active part and without them many cub packs would simply have disappeared.
