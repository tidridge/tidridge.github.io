INTRODUCING REV. DUANE I. BERRY, Pastor Highlands Baptist Church.
 

 

             Rev. Duane Berry was born and raised in Moncton, New Brunswick. He attended the United Baptist Bible School during which time he was licensed to preach by his home Church, Highfield Baptist Church. He attend-ed Cordon College majoring in Christian Education. He was active in athletics, music and student government, being President of the student body in his senior year. Following graduation in 1963, he attended Gordon - Conwell Seminary and served as student Pastor of a small Covenant Church. Graduating in 1967, he became Minister of Education at Ruggles Baptist Church, Boston, Mass. Then for three yea::s was Minister of Education & Youth at Grace Chapel in Lexington, Mass. In 1971, he became pastor of Trinity Baptist Church, Nashua, N. H.

              Sandra (Rile) and Duane met in college and were married in 1964. Sandra received her B.S. in Elemen-tary Education from Gordon College. She is a native of flue Bell, Pa.(Philadelphia area). She has had an active ministry through her ventriloquism and music, both singing and playing the piano and teaching.

               The Berrys have four children, Barclay 11, Bradley 10, Stephanie 8 and Brett 4.

The name of the church is the Neighbourhood Church (Baptist) of Edmonton, Alberta in

affiliation with the Canadian Baptists of Western Canada

It is registered under the Religious Societies Act (#540011079)

 

 

CONSTITUTION

 

Mission Statement:

 

We are a church for the neighbourhood, growing healthy and whole
in: Worship, Formation and Mission.

 

Scriptural Authority:

 

We believe that the Bible is the sufficient and authoritative revelation of God in
everything affecting our faith and our practice, and obedience to its teachings is binding
upon all Christians (II Timothy 3-16-17).

[For further reference and discussion on theological matters, we apply the theological standard
(as affirmed by the Baptist Union of Western Canada) set out in Christian Theology:
Millard J. Erickson 1983, Baker Book House.]

 

Confession:

 

We confess (believe, live, practice, and experience) that:

 

Jesus Christ is the Creator and Saviour of the World and deserves
our unending devotion as Lord (John 1: 1-5);

 

Our devotion to Christ impacts every dimension of our
personal conduct +(Ephesians 5:1-4; 15-17);

 

Christ has given the Church to be the people of God in the world (John 17:6-8, 26) so
the Church is to advance the Kingdom of God in the world (Matthew 28:19-20).

 

We believe the core doctrines found in the Apostles' Creed:

Creator of heaven and earth.

I believe in Jesus Christ,
God’s only Son, our Lord;

Who was conceived by the Holy Spirit,

crucified, died, and was buried.
He descended into hell.

judge the living and the dead.

I believe in the Holy Spirit,

The communion of saints,

The forgiveness of sins,

The resurrection of the body,

And the life everlasting.

Amen.

 

Baptism:

 

We practise baptism by immersion as was the practise of the New Testament.

 

Baptism is a confession of faith. Each baptism expresses the faith of the candidate as well as that of the church. Baptism is also an oath of solemn obligation. When people are baptised with an understanding of its meaning and significance, they are expressing a vow that they will walk with the church in newness of life. The act of Baptism is a powerful witness to all those who observe it. (Matthew 3: 13-17)

 

 

Communion:

 

Communion is both a memorial and a symbol. It is observed in remembrance of Jesus, His life, His death, His resurrection, His words and His works. It is a visible expression of the Gospel and it is in the light of the Gospel that it is understood. The Bread and the Wine speak of Christ's Body and Blood, the price He so willingly paid for our salvation.

 

Communion is the presence of Christ, mediated through believing faith, which transforms an act of remembrance into a service of communion. (1 Corinthians 11:23-26)

 

Spiritual Democracy:

 

We believe in religious liberty, including the principle of congregational church government, with the members of the church having equal rights, privileges and duties under the Lordship of Christ.

 

BYLAWS

 

 

Church Structure

 

The Neighbourhood Church consists of members and is governed by a Church Board with pastoral staff and several Church officials, as described below. All Elders, pastoral staff, and Church officials must be members of the Church and are elected by the Church members.
They must be members in good standing and role models in their ethical and moral conduct and behaviour, and in their participation in the life of the Church.

 

  1. Members

 

A Church member in good standing meets the membership requirements set out
by the Bylaws, and lives by biblical principles and the principles laid out in the
Church Constitution.

 

  1. Board of Elders

 

    1. The Board of Elders, also known as the Church Board, Elders Board, or the Board, consists of at least five Elders, one being the Chair, one being the Senior Pastor, and
      one being the Finance Elder.

 

    1. Elders (except for the Senior Pastor) are elected for a three-year term and are directly accountable to the Church. The terms for the Elders are overlapping, with the terms of one or two Elders ending each year. When a vacancy occurs before a term is expired, the replacing Elder completes the term.

 

  1. Elders

 

    1. An Elder is a church official elected by Church members to have spiritual oversight of the Church, to serve on the Board of Elders, to make decisions, and to provide leadership in the Church.

 

    1. An Elder is elected based on being respected by the congregation as a person of wisdom, character, and spiritual maturity. He or she is a proven leader in the Church, is able to contribute well to the decision-making of the Church with their gifts and abilities, and fully participate in the life of the Church.

 

 

  1. Senior Pastor

 

    1. The Senior Pastor is the under-shepherd of the congregation under the Lordship of Jesus Christ, and is the Elder who ministers the Word. The Senior Pastor’s major task is to proclaim the Gospel of Jesus Christ, minister to the spiritual needs of the congregation and to assist in training the congregation to use their spiritual gifts for the work of the ministry in the world. It is the Senior Pastor's role to provide inspiration and leadership in all phases of church life. Every effort should be made to relieve the Senior Pastor of onerous details that prevent important tasks from being carried out

 

    1. The Senior Pastor, by the virtue of his or her position, is a member of the Church and
      a member of the Church Board.

 

    1. The Senior Pastor is an ex-officio member of all Church committees, sub-committees and task forces.

 

    1. The Senior Pastor:

      1. provides spiritual leadership to the Church and works in conjunction with the Church Board to plan spiritual leadership and then to implement it with other staff members and Church lay people;

      2. works in conjunction with the Church Board in formulating and recommending policy;

      3. exercises general supervisory authority over all staff members of the Church (excluding staffing changes); and

      4. has the right to be notified of all meetings of the Church Board and to be present and fully participate at all such meetings.

  1. Associate Pastors

 

    1. Associate Pastors may be called by the Church for the purpose of undertaking specific ministries as the Senior Pastor and the Church Board determine necessary for the Church.

 

    1. Associate Pastors, by virtue of their position, are members of the Church but not members of the Church Board.

 

    1. Associate Pastors are accountable to the Senior Pastor.

 

  1. Other Staff Members

 

Other staff may be hired by the Church Board for the purpose of ensuring specific tasks are implemented as the Senior Pastor and Church Board determine are necessary for the Church.

 

 

  1. The Moderator

 

    1. The Moderator is a church official who presides over all Church meetings, calls all Church meetings, and with the Church Clerk and Board Chair, establishes the agenda. The Moderator is also the final interpreter of the Bylaws, and can be referred to as
      a last resort to resolve disputes.

 

    1. The Moderator is elected by the Church members for a three-year term and is responsible directly to the Church.

 

  1. The Church Clerk

 

    1. The Church Clerk is a church official who keeps minutes of all Church meetings,
      keeps records of correspondence involving the business of the Church, and
      preserves Church records.

 

    1. The Church Clerk is elected by the Church members and is directly responsible to
      the Church.

 

  1. The Stewardship Secretary

 

The Stewardship Secretary is a church official who is in charge of the record of envelope offerings, memorial gifts and other financial gifts made to the Church where an acknowledgement or receipt is required. The Stewardship Secretary is elected by the Church members for a three-year term, and is responsible to the Finance Elder.

 

 

 

Operating Procedures

 

  1. Method of Support

 

The Church receives financial support primarily by the voluntary offering of its members and adherents.

 

  1. Membership

 

  1. A church member is any person who has signed The Neighbourhood Church Membership Covenant (Appendix 1) for the current year. Church members have the right to vote at Church meetings and only Church members can be Church officials.
     

  2. Membership is defined by a commitment to the Membership Covenant. This commitment is renewed by signing a copy of Appendix 1. A record of the signed copies will by kept by the Church Clerk.
     

  3. An annual Membership Service is held for renewal of Church membership by re-signing the Membership Covenant. Those unable to attend the service, or new congregants, are able to request and receive membership by committing to the Membership Covenant with Church Elder acting as witness.

i)Members of the Church prior to June 2014 may retain their ongoing membership at the discretion of the Elders’ Board under the heading “Grandfathered.”
If and when they decide to participate in the covenant membership process, their membership will need to be renewed each year as outlined in 2b).

 

  1. Those unable to participate in church activities due to health or disability, but wish to continue their connection with the church, may be made Lifetime Members. The list of Lifetime Members is reviewed and maintained by the Elders’ Board and Church Clerk. Lifetime members are non-voting members and are not part of the calculation of quorum at Church meetings.
     

  2. The Church can only terminate a member’s membership by a 75% majority vote (excluding blanks and abstentions) at a Church meeting. A Church member can terminate their membership in writing.

 

  1. Ministries in the Church

 

  1. The day-to-day ministries of the Church are coordinated by staff members and Church lay people under the direction of the Senior Pastor, based on approved direction from the Church Board.
     

  2. The Senior Pastor is directly accountable to the Church Board for all ministries within the Church.

 

  1. Board of Elders Meetings

 

  1. The Board of Elders holds regular meetings. Any Church member or adherent can attend a meeting of the Church Board, unless confidential matters are being discussed.

 

  1. A quorum for a meeting of the Board is two less than the total number of Board members, with a minimum of three.

 

  1. Each year, the Church Board selects an Elder to be chair. The Senior Pastor cannot serve as chair.

 

 

  1. Committees and Task Forces

 

  1. The Personnel Committee

The Personnel Committee meets as needed to make recommendations to the Church Board concerning hours, salaries, and benefits for staff. The Committee also meets if the need arises to resolve issues raised by staff members. The Committee reports to the Church Board and consists of the Chair of the Church Board, the Finance Elder, the Senior Pastor, and a member-at-large elected by the Church members. The Committee is chaired by the Chair of the Church Board. Where a conflict between any of the paid staff and the Senior Pastor cannot be resolved, the matter is referred to the Personnel Committee for resolution.

 

  1. The Nominating Committee

The Nominating Committee meets as needed to recommend members for Church official positions. The Committee consists of two Elders, appointed by the Church Board, and three members at large, elected by the Church members for a three-year term. The Committee selects a committee member to be chair.
 

  1. Pastoral Selection Task Forces

A Pastoral Selection Task Force is formed, as needed, to recommend a candidate for the position of Senior Pastor or Associate Pastor. The membership of the Task Force is recommended by the Church Board and approved by the Church members. It includes at least two Elders, the Sr. Pastor, and at least two members at large. The Task Force selects a task force member to be chair.

 

  1. Church Meetings and Reports

 

  1. Church meetings are held for matters requiring approval of the Church members.

 

  1. Church meetings may be held any time of the year. They are called by the Moderator at the request of the Church Board or Nominating Committee, or by a written request signed by at least ten Church members. The dates and times for Church meetings are decided by the Moderator in consultation with the Chair of the Church Board.

 

  1. The date, time, and agenda for Church meetings, as well as available motions and documents for approval, are posted at least one month in advance for changes to the Constitution and Bylaws and at least two weeks in advance otherwise.

 

  1. The quorum for a Church meeting is 20% of the first 100 active members and 10% of the remaining active members.

 

 

  1. An annual Church meeting is held between April 15th and June 30th.

 

  1. At this meeting, the budget for the next fiscal year is approved.

 

  1. At this meeting, the annual report for the previous Ministry year is approved. The report includes submissions from the Senior Pastor on the ministries in the Church, a submission from the chair of the Church Board, and a submission from the Church Clerk.

 

  1. Annual reports from the Finance Elder, the Auditors, and the Stewardship Secretary will be published between September 15thst and December 31st.

 

  1. Where neither the Constitution nor the Bylaws apply, then the Rules of Order for all Church meetings is the most recent edition of Bourinot's Rules of Order.

 

  1. Finances

 

  1. The Church’s fiscal year and the Church’s ministry year runs from September 1st to August 31st.

 

  1. The Church Clerk, the Chair of the Church Board, and the Finance Elder, exercise the powers of the Church in matters pertaining to Church property. Upon instruction of the Church, they can sign all official documents and provide for their security.

 

  1. The Finance Elder is responsible for the following financial tasks:

 

  1. compiling and presenting the annual budget to the Church;

 

  1. establishing and implementing all Church financial policies, procedures, and financial controls, in accordance with generally accepted accounting principles, and in consultation with the Church Board and the Church;

 

  1. ensuring through regular appraisals that appropriate fire and liability insurance coverage is maintained for Church employees, volunteers and properties; and,

 

  1. providing regular financial and stewardship information to the Church through monthly and annual financial assessments and reporting, through communicating proper methods and procedures for use of Church financial and physical resources, and through stewardship emphasis programs.

 

  1. The Stewardship Secretary is responsible for keeping an accurate record of all monies received through the envelope system and supplies each contributor a statement of giving at the end of the fiscal year. With the exception of summary reports, any knowledge acquired and all materials prepared by the Stewardship Secretary are confidential and are not released or disclosed to any party other than the individual envelope holder.

 

  1. The Church's financial books for the current year are reviewed by two people appointed as auditors. The auditors report in writing to the Church. The auditors are nominated by the Nominating Committee and approved by the Church members.

 

  1. Policy Development

 

Policy matters involving the operational procedures of the Church are referred in writing from staff members and Church lay people, under the direction of the Senior Pastor, to the Church Board for approval.
 

  1. Changes to Staff

 

  1. The Senior Pastor (in consultation with the Personnel Committee) determines staffing needs and presents proposals concerning changes in staff hours and salary to the Church Board for approval (including details of any new staff positions, time frame and cost implications). The Church Board then takes the proposal to the Church for approval.

 

  1. For the calling of a new Senior Pastor or Associate Pastor, a Pastoral Selection Task Force is formed to review potential candidates and recommend a candidate to the Church. Church members then vote to approve the hiring of a new Senior Pastor or Associate Pastor. The Task Force follows the pastoral selection guidelines of the Canadian Baptists of Western Canada while reviewing candidates.

 

  1. The Senior Pastor, an Associate Pastor, or a Church official can be removed from his or her position only after a majority vote of the Church Board and a 75% majority vote (excluding blanks and abstentions) at a Church meeting.

 

  1. Changes to Church Officials

 

The Nominating Committee nominates members to fill vacant or soon-to-be-vacant Church official positions (striving to fill positions prior to completion of the term of office). The chair provides a list of candidates to the Church Clerk at least two weeks prior to a Church meeting. Church members may nominate additional candidates before voting on the nominations.

 

 

  1. Reviewing and Amending the Constitution and Bylaws

 

  1. Amendments to the Constitution or Bylaws are submitted by the Church Board to the Church members for approval. Approval requires a 75% majority vote (excluding blanks and abstentions) at a Church meeting.

 

  1. Proposed amendments are posted in writing at least one month in advance. For comparison purposes, the current text is posted along with the amendments.

 

  1. The Constitution and Bylaws are reviewed at least every five years. The revision date is based on the date the last change was adopted, or the previous revision date.

 

  1. Minor errors relating to spelling, punctuation, or transcribing can be corrected by the unanimous vote of the Church Board. These corrections are reported to the Church.
